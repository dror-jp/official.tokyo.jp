ja_term: "住民税"
layout: "procedure"
summary: "The local tax that funds your ward. It is billed to whoever lived in Japan on January 1, based on the previous year's income — which is why the first bill surprises people in year two."
category: "money-tax"
who: "Anyone with a registered address in Japan on January 1 who earned above the taxable threshold in the previous year. The ward where you lived on January 1 is the one that bills you — even if you move later."
deadline: "Bills arrive in June"
cost: "Based on last year's income"
place: "Via employer, or payment slips"
steps:
  - title: "Check how you will pay"
    text: "Company employees: your employer deducts it from your salary monthly (special collection) — nothing to do. Everyone else: a payment notice arrives by post around June."
  - title: "Understand the timing"
    text: "The tax is calculated on your income from January to December of the previous year. That is why a bill can arrive after your income changed — or after you changed jobs."
  - title: "Pay by the deadlines on the slips"
    text: "At banks, post offices, convenience stores, or the ward office. Direct debit can also be set up."

faq:
  - q: "I just arrived in Japan — why haven't I received a bill?"
    a: "Resident tax is calculated on the previous year's income in Japan. If you had no income in Japan last year, there is nothing to bill yet — most newcomers see their first bill in their second year."
  - q: "I moved to another ward in March. Who bills me?"
    a: "The ward where you lived on January 1 bills you for the whole year, even if you moved away afterwards."
  - q: "Is this the same as income tax?"
    a: "No. Income tax is national and is withheld as you earn. Resident tax is local, and is billed one year behind."
